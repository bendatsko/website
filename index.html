Benjamin Datsko

I am an undergraduate at the University of Michigan pursuing a BSE in Computer Science. I am interested in hardware and lower-level systems, particularly embedded software and robotics.

I am currently a Research Assistant in the Michigan Integrated Circuits Lab's Flynn Research Group and a Model-Based Design Engineering Intern at Otis Elevator Company.

Research

I am interested in edge computing, high-performance mixed-signal integrated circuits, neumorphic computing, and optimization. Most of my research is in using noise-reduction techniques to improve performance and efficiency of ASICs.

A 64-Element Fully Integrated 28GHz Digital Beamformer with In-Package 16x16 Patch Antenna Array and 128 Continuous-Time Band-Pass Delta-Sigma Sub-ADCs

Beamforming allows devices transmitting signals to point in a particular dimension. With analog beamforming you can only listen to one thing at a time, but with digital beamforming you can switch quickly between them. This might be useful, for example, on drones sent into disaster areas in order to provide emergency internet to those in trouble.

Low-Cost 20 GHz to 40 GHz Modulated Signal Source

Combining the AD9174 FPGA, ADS7 DAC, and ADMV1013 broadband up-converter enables the generation of any sort of sequence (single tone, QAM, etc.) at any frequency between 20 GHz and 40 GHz.
