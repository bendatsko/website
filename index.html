Research

I often work with mixed-signal integrated circuits, PCB design, and embedded software. In-progress projects that build upon past papers are represented by their most recent publication and are marked with an asterisk.

*An 8-bit 20.7 TOPS/W Multi-Level Cell ReRAM-based Compute Engine
Justin M. Correll, Lu Jie, Seungheun Song, Seungjong Lee, Junkang Zhu, Wei Tang, Luke Wormald, Jack Erhardt, Nicolas Breil, Roger Quon, Deepak Kamalanathan, Siddarth Krishnan, Michael Chudzik, Zhengya Zhang, Wei D. Lu, Michael P. Flynn
2022 IEEE Symposium on VLSI Technology and Circuits (VLSI Technology and Circuits)

An ASIC designed for accelerating scientific computing and machine learning tasks in edge computing and IoT devices. Integrates self-contained ReRAM-based compute-in-memory (CIM) tiles with a RISC-V host, delivering high efficiency and compute density.

*A 16-Element Fully Integrated 28GHz Digital Beamformer with In-Package 4x4 Patch Antenna Array and 64 Continuous-Time Band-Pass Delta-Sigma Sub-ADCs
Rundao Lu, Christine Weston, Daniel Weyer, Fred Buhler, Michael P. Flynn
2020 IEEE Radio Frequency Integrated Circuits Symposium (RFIC)

A 28GHz digital beamformer integrated circuit featuring a 4x4 patch antenna array capable of generating multiple beams simultaneously for mm-wave communication.

Modulated Signal Source from ADI Components for Versatile Frequency Generation from 20GHz to 40GHz
Hao Yu, Christine Weston, Abraham Lopez, Benjamin Datsko, Michael P. Flynn

A signal generator incorporating an FPGA, high-frequency RF DAC, and high-frequency broadband up converter. A MATLAB script generates loop-able signal sequences (16-QAM, 64-QAM, single-tone, etc.) run by the FPGA, converted to an analog equivalent, and up-converted from 100 MHz to radio-frequency (28 GHz).

Ultra-Low Noise 5V Power Supply for Noise-Sensitive Testing Leveraging LiPo Batteries
Benjamin Datsko, Luke Wormald, Michael P. Flynn

A 5V power supply designed for noise-sensitive integrated circuit testing. The use of battery cells eliminates high-frequency switching noise. Further filtering on low-inductance 5V plane provided by ferrite bead for high frequencies. 5V LDO with a high power supply rejection ratio further reduces output ripple (~3mV Pk-Pk). Employs ATmega32U4 microcontroller and C++-based RTOS for BMS management, sensor reading, and display updates.
